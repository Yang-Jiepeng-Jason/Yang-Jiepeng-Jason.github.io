l  Water cycle

n  The three main storage areas of water are oceans and seas / atmosphere / land

n  The movement of water in the three main storage areas called water cycle

n  Different process in a water cycle

Process

Meaning

Evaporation

When the sun heats the earth’s surface, the water in seas, rivers, lakes and soil evaporates and becomes water vapour. Water vapour rises up into the air

Transpiration

Moisture is released from plants as water vapour. Water vapour rises up into the air

Condensation

When water vapour rises, it cools and condenses into water droplets.

These water droplets group together and form clouds.

Precipitation

The clouds get heavy and water falls back to the earth’s surface in the form of rain or snow

Overland flow

Water flows on the land surface

Infiltration

Water seeps into the ground and becomes groundwater

l  Precipitation in China

(REFER to OXFORD Junior Secondary Exploring Geography 4 The trouble of water P.15-16(fig.8a, 8b))

n  In general, the amount of precipitation in China decreases from south-east to north-west.

n  Monsoons climate

u  A large part of China has a monsoon climate.

u  Monsoons are winds that change directions seasonally, blowing from one direction in summer and from the opposite direction in winter.

u  Onshore winds (south-west and south-east monsoons) blow from the sea to the southern and eastern parts to Asia.

u  In summer, the land absorbs heat from the sun quickly. The air above becomes hot and rises. Air pressure over the land is low. Water absorbs heat more slowly than the land, so the air over the sea is cooler. Cool air sinks. Air pressure over the sea is higher. Air moves from high-pressure areas to low-pressure areas. Therefore, winds blow from the sea towards the southern and eastern parts of Asia. Winds blowing from the sea to the land are onshore winds. They are the summer monsoons. They carry much moisture and bring the coastal regions wet summers.

u  Offshore winds (north, north-west and north-east monsoons) blow from the land to the sea

u  In winter, the land loses heat quickly, so that the land surface is cold. Cold air sinks. Air pressure over the land is higher. Water loses heat more slowly than the land, so the sore over the sea is warmer. Warm air roses. Air pressure over the sea is lower. Air moves from high-pressure areas to lower-pressure areas. Therefore, winds blow from the land towards the sea. Wings blowing from the land to the sea are offshore winds. They are the winter monsoons. They are dry. Therefore areas affected by winter monsoons in China are dry.

n  Distance from the sea

u  During summer monsoons in China, the onshore blow from the sea, carry much moisture. When the wet winds reach the inland stead, the moisture is lost. Therefore, the eastern coastal regions receive more precipitation than the inland regions.

u  The climatic graph shows the monthly mean temperature (red line) and monthly rainfall(blue bars a place.

u  Annual range of temperature: highest monthly mean temperature C lowest monthly mean temperature

u  Annual rainfall: sun of the monthly rainfall

n  Relationship between precipitation and freshwater resources and China’s water problems

u  With the monsoon climate, the amount of precipitation received in a given place varies with the seasons

u  In China, the areas affected by the monsoon climate receive a higher rainfall in summer than in winter.

u  The distribution of freshwater resources is therefore highly uneven over time. In the wet season, the volume of river flow is higher. More freshwater resources are available during the wet season (summer) than the dry season (winter).

u  If the dry season is longer than normal, drought may occur.

u  If the rainfall in the wet season is exceptionally high, flooding may occur.
